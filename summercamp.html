Sports

Nyasa concluded a successful summer camp for 95 kids from the nearby villages and IITGN construction worker colonies. A ten days program was scheduled, which included a variety of activities.

Students were divided into four groups, each playing the following games: dodgeball, chess, squash, and badminton. Following this, they also played some interesting games like ball tunnel and Kangaroo jump.

In the ball tunnel, players were divided into two teams and lined up in a straight line facing the same direction with their legs standing apart to form a tunnel. The player at the end of the line turns to face the other way and stands with their knees bent and their head down, looking for the ball as it comes through the legs.

In the Kangaroo jump, players were divided into two groups. Players would hop like a Kangaroo while balancing a ball between their knees to finish the race.

Arts & Crafts

A brief craft session was conducted for 60 minutes and covered several interesting concepts on paper folding techniques to create beautiful origami products. By the end of the session, every participant was able to make an origami tulip flower.

The painting session was focused on exploring the concepts of basic artwork with mixed media. The children used various mediums/materials like paint, colored paper, buttons, and vegetable and used techniques like vegetable printing, painting, etc to create an artwork as a team. By engaging the children in this group activity, they participated in team-building exercises and leadership skills parallelly.

Music

The music session was conducted by Sargam, the music club at IIT Gandhinagar. The team included singers Chinmayee Shukla, Akshat Khanna, and Praanshu Sharan with Harvansh Dandelia on the guitar. The session was widely appreciated by the children with active participation that gave it a concert feel. The children were given a chance at playing percussion instruments while playing a game of antakshari. The session concluded with the children singing their favourite songs with the band.

Dancing

The dance session was by far the most exciting one, with several children, especially girls, who were highly enthusiastic about participating. It was conducted in five sessions. The first class was an introduction to the basic hook steps of Bollywood songs.

In the second class, girls were introduced to a semi-classical form of dance along with their Bollywood dance. Several kids were excited, they actively participated in rehearsals, and were always ready to learn new steps and choreography. On the final day of the summer camp, the children did a fabulous dance performance using the dance learned during sessions.

Tree Plantaion

In honour of World Environment Day, the students at Nyasa Summer Camp were engaged in a tree plantation drive on the campus. The children were grouped and given a sapling to plant. An informative and interactive session was conducted following the plantation drive by Prof. CN Pandey, a faculty member from the Earth Sciences department. Prof. Pandey spoke to the students about the local flora and fauna, the impending threat to our ecosystem, and the possible ways in which it can be prevented. The enthusiastic children asked questions and sought more explanations on many topics.

Archaeology

This day-long session gave the children and volunteers a glimpse of the life of an archaeologist through sandbox mock excavations. Further, the session also gave them an idea of how artifacts from the excavation sites are assembled and analysed through a simple exercise of pot painting, breaking, and joining. The participants were first shown pictures from different ancient cultures. They were then asked to recreate the same design (for those inclined to paint) or paint anything that they wanted to.

After the painting, the pots were deliberately broken to resemble findings from the excavation fields. Finally, the participants were tasked with rejoining the broken pots from another.

Library

The children were taken on a tour to visit the Library on campus in batches of four. After a brief tour of the Library, the kids were asked to pick up a book they found interesting from the Children Collection. Many already had a specific genre in mind, like horror or botany, and wanted to pick books on the same. Others browsed through the collection and picked what caught their eyes, which were mainly the ones with colourful illustration.

Once they finished flipping through those, many asked for books in Hindi or Gujarati. The Hindi collection was introduced, and the excited crowd picked up books of their choices. They sat on the sofas in the lobby area and were intrigued to keep reading until it was time to go.

Computer Class

The computer session was conducted in four batches and in three steps:

    1) An introduction to hardware, software, input device, output device, storage device, and different parts of the computer
    2) A guide to switching the computer on and off.
    3) Basic introduction about paint and how to use it to make colourful drawings.

Language

The children were given a formal introduction to the English language and were taught basic phrases that would be useful for them within the campus as well as in their daily lives.

  • Greeting a person - “Hi” “Hello”
  • Responding - “Thank you” “Welcome”
  • Asking questions - “May I..?”
  • Answering - “I like..?”

    The sessions conducted by the centre for creative learning (CCL) were by far the most exciting ones for the children and volunteers alike. The team introduced science and math topics through fun little activities and instruments such as:

  • Straw flute - To understand the generation and movement of sound by constructing a flute or whistle with straw.
  • Cartesian diver- A DIY lie detector that works on the gas laws and the relation between pressure and volume.
  • Graduation cap - Crafty and recycled activity to make origami caps from newspapers.
  • Machine syringe - A fun way to demonstrate machines.
  • Paper helicopter - An introduction to air resistance and torque with a fun paper toy.
  • Straw Spiner - To demonstrate Newton's laws of motion with a spinner.
